Executive Summary

The Problem

Real estate agents on Spain's Costa Blanca spend 20 to 30 minutes writing one generic listing description, paste it unchanged across every portal, and skip French, German, and Dutch entirely. In one of Europe's most international property markets, that means reaching 30 to 40% of the available buyer pool.

The Solution

A single textarea input triggers 29 parallel API calls, each generating portal-specific, culturally-adapted copy for a distinct portal/language combination. Output is ready to publish in under 60 seconds, with a 100-point quality score and interactive gap-filling to improve it further.

Bottom Line

10 paying agencies at €99/month reached €990 MRR at 3 months post-launch, acquired at zero CAC. The limiting factor is distribution, not the technology or unit economics.

02

The Pain Point

Market Reality

The northern Costa Blanca (Jávea, Moraira, Calpe, Benidorm) is one of Europe's most international property markets. Buyers are British, French, German, Dutch, and Belgian, each using different portals with different formatting rules, character limits, and audience expectations. A 10-agent agency producing 20 listings per month writes one generic description in one language, pastes it unchanged everywhere, and skips the rest. That is 7 to 10 hours per month on a task done poorly.

Cost of Inaction

Listings that only exist in Spanish and broken English reach 30 to 40% of the international buyer pool. French, German, and Dutch buyers never see them. Listings without English on ThinkSpain are invisible to 100% of that portal's audience. Properties listed in a buyer's native language with portal-optimized formatting receive measurably higher engagement on Idealista and Kyero.

Why AI, Not Templates

The structural rules are deterministic, character limits, paragraph orders, legal requirements. The part that breaks template-based approaches is cultural adaptation: a German buyer expects m2, energy certificate, and build year first; a French buyer expects the lifestyle lead; a Dutch buyer wants price per m2. Templates cannot route cultural voice per language per portal. A model with per-language system prompts and per-portal structural constraints can.

No RAG. No fine-tuning. No vector database. The entire system runs on prompt engineering, few-shot examples, and deterministic post-processing, chosen deliberately: a new failure mode means a regex fix deployed in minutes, not a model retrain deployed in days.

Solution & PM Strategy

Product Hypothesis

"If we generate per-portal, per-language descriptions with cultural voice routing and deterministic quality gates, agencies will save 90%+ of listing creation time while producing higher-quality output than they could write manually, because cultural adaptation and portal-specific optimization are the failure modes of current agent behaviour, not effort."

The Core Challenge

Making an LLM write listings is trivial. The challenge was preventing it from adding information. Spanish real estate listings are legal documents. An invented "5-minute walk to the beach" that is actually 15 minutes creates liability. The entire architecture exists to enforce one constraint: every word in the output must trace to a specific fact in the input.

Hard-Won Lessons

Three Pivots

Single prompt for all portals and languages: scrapped. Produced generic copy that optimized for nothing. Moved to 1 call per portal per language. API cost increased 5 to 10x. Quality justified it at ~€0.01 per generation.

Reactive prohibition patching: scrapped at v8. 30+ sub-prohibitions competing for model attention, scores oscillating without converging. Principle-based rewrite (3 rules) broke the plateau in one iteration.

LLM reviewer as quality gate: scrapped. Inconsistent scores across runs on identical outputs. Replaced with deterministic regression suite as the hard gate. LLM reviewer retained for discovery only.

Ethics & Governance

Factual accuracy is the product's core legal constraint. Spanish real estate listings are legal documents. No PII processed: the system ingests property descriptions (public data) and outputs marketing copy. GDPR compliance via architecture: Supabase RLS enforces per-user data isolation. No training on user data, standard Anthropic API data processing terms apply.

What I'd Do Differently

Build the regression suite and audit scripts at v1, not v7. Every prompt change should be gated by a deterministic test suite from day one. Months were spent on subjective quality assessment that a 15-test regression suite would have replaced in one afternoon.
