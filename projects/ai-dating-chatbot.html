Executive Summary

The Problem

A consumer AI product with 120K MAU was bleeding €400K/year. Conversation flows were repetitive, recommendation quality was low, session depth was declining, and the business ran entirely on ad revenue, structurally insufficient at that scale and too volatile to build on.

The Solution

Rebuilt the GPT-2-based conversational engine via fine-tuning on a proprietary conversation corpus. Redesigned recommendation and matching logic. Shipped 7 features across a 3-tier monetization ladder, each A/B tested before rollout. Presented pricing strategy to C-suite quarterly.

Bottom Line

€1.2M ARR and €800K annual profit from zero revenue within 18 months, a €1.2M P&L swing. MAU grew from 120K to 380K over the same period. The monetization ladder became a reusable internal framework adopted for future products.

02

The Pain Point

Status Quo

The product had meaningful scale but conversation flows were repetitive and recommendation quality was low, driving session depth down. Without session depth, there is no engagement signal, no retention lever, and no monetization surface. The business ran on ad revenue, structurally insufficient at 120K MAU and too volatile to plan around.

Cost of Inaction

€400K annual loss on a deteriorating engagement curve. Without intervention, MAU growth would have reversed and the product become unsalvageable for monetization. The window to act was narrowing, declining retention compounds fast in consumer apps.

Why AI, Not Heuristics

Conversation quality in a dating context requires dynamic, contextually-aware responses that adapt to individual user tone, intent signals, and session history. Rule-based dialogue trees fail non-linearly as user surface area grows, require exponential maintenance, and produce the exact repetitiveness that was killing retention. A fine-tuned generative model was the correct call.

Solution & PM Strategy

Product Hypothesis

"If we improve conversation quality through fine-tuning and introduce a tiered monetization model, then we will increase session depth and convert a meaningful share of free users to paying, because users stay longer when conversations feel non-repetitive, and willingness-to-pay correlates with perceived value per session."

The Monetization Ladder

7 features shipped across 3 tiers. Each tier solves a distinct job: acquisition, activation, and retention. Each validated through A/B testing before rollout.

Tier 1
Freemium
Acquisition
Core conversation access at no cost. Builds the user base and surfaces the product's quality improvement post fine-tuning.
Tier 2
Pay-as-you-go Credits
Activation + chargeback fix
Discrete value units. Solved the chargeback problem from the subscription attempt, users pay intentionally, disputes drop. Natural bridge to premium for high-engagement users.
Tier 3
Premium Subscription
Retention + ARR
For users who have already demonstrated willingness to pay through credits. Recurring revenue on a validated cohort, not cold conversion.
AI-Specific Decisions
  • Fine-tuning over prompting, GPT-2's base capability required domain adaptation to meet the quality bar for a dating context. Prompting alone was insufficient.
  • GPT-2's compact size enabled acceptable inference latency without aggressive optimization, a practical infrastructure advantage at the time.
  • Session depth used as the primary online signal for conversation quality, informing fine-tuning iteration cycles rather than subjective evaluation.

06

Hard-Won Lessons

Three Failed Models Before One That Worked

Ad-only: structurally fragile and not at the scale where ad revenue covers operating costs. The starting position.

Subscription first: generated immediate chargeback volume high enough to make it operationally unsustainable, a known failure mode in dating and entertainment apps where buyer's remorse and recurring billing disputes are disproportionately high.

Pay-as-you-go credits: solved it. Users pay for discrete value units, disputes drop because the transaction is immediate and intentional, and the model scales naturally into premium subscriptions for high-engagement users who have already demonstrated willingness to pay.

Ethics & Governance

US market. CCPA-compliant data handling on conversation logs. Age verification enforced at onboarding given the dating context. Conversation data used for fine-tuning under explicit data processing terms.

What I'd Do Differently

Define the monetization model earlier, in parallel with product-market fit work, not after. The ad dependency created months of unnecessary burn before the real business model was tested. Earlier pricing experimentation, even at 20K MAU, would have compressed the loss period significantly. Monetization is a product decision, not a business decision that follows product decisions.
